Instagram Design Sizes 2025

Instagram Posts

  • Square Posts: 1080 × 1080 pixels
  • Landscape Posts: 1080 × 566 pixels
  • Portrait Posts: 1080 × 1350 pixels
  • Carousel Posts: 1080 × 1080 pixels (recommended)

Instagram Stories & Reels

  • Stories: 1080 × 1920 pixels (9:16 aspect ratio)
  • Reels: 1080 × 1920 pixels (9:16 aspect ratio)
  • Reels Cover: 1080 × 1920 pixels

Instagram Profile & Headers

  • Profile Picture: 320 × 320 pixels (displays at 110 × 110)
  • Story Highlights: 1080 × 1920 pixels
Pro Tip: Instagram compresses images, so always start with high-resolution designs. Tools like LayoutCraft automatically optimize for platform compression while maintaining quality.

Facebook Design Sizes 2025

Facebook Posts

  • Portrait Posts: 1080 × 1350 pixels

Facebook Ads

  • Feed Ads: 1080 × 1080 pixels (square) or 1200 × 628 pixels (landscape)
  • Story Ads: 1080 × 1920 pixels

Facebook Business Page

  • Profile Picture: 320 × 320 pixels

LinkedIn Design Sizes 2025

LinkedIn Posts

  • Standard Posts: 1200 × 628 pixels

LinkedIn Company Page

  • Cover Image: 1536 × 768 pixels
  • Logo: 300 × 300 pixels

LinkedIn Ads

  • Message Ads: 300 × 250 pixels

LinkedIn Tip: Professional platforms favor clean, text-readable designs. Avoid cluttered visuals that work on Instagram but fail on LinkedIn's business-focused audience.

Multi-Platform Design Strategy

1. Start with Your Hero Size

Choose your primary platform's dimensions as your base design. For most marketers, this is either:

  • Instagram square (1080 × 1080) for B2C brands
  • LinkedIn landscape (1200 × 628) for B2B companies

2. Design for Adaptation

  • Keep important text and logos in the center 60% of your design
  • Use backgrounds that can extend or crop gracefully
  • Avoid placing crucial elements at edges

3. Use Smart Tools

Modern AI design tools can create multiple formats from a single prompt. Instead of manually resizing, describe your campaign once and generate all formats simultaneously. For tips on this, read our guide on writing better design prompts.

Design Quality Checklist

Before publishing any social media design, verify:

Text Readability

  • [ ] Text readable at thumbnail size
  • [ ] High contrast between text and background
  • [ ] Font size minimum 24px for social media
  • [ ] Important information in safe zones

Brand Consistency

  • [ ] Logo placement consistent across formats
  • [ ] Brand colors accurately represented
  • [ ] Typography matches brand guidelines
  • [ ] Overall style consistent with other content

Technical Quality

  • [ ] High resolution (at least 72 DPI)
  • [ ] Correct dimensions for platform
  • [ ] File size under platform limits
  • [ ] Proper file format (JPG for photos, PNG for graphics)

Common Social Media Design Mistakes

1. One-Size-Fits-All Approach

Using the same image across all platforms results in poor performance. Each platform has different user behavior and optimal dimensions.

2. Ignoring Mobile Display

Over 80% of social media consumption happens on mobile. Designs that look great on desktop often fail on small screens.

3. Text-Heavy Designs

Social media users scroll quickly. Designs with too much text get ignored. Keep copy concise and impactful.

4. Inconsistent Branding

Different designs for each platform confuses your audience. Maintain consistent visual identity while adapting to platform requirements.
